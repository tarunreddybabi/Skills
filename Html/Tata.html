RATAN TATA

Ratan Naval Tata (born 28 December 1937) is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist and former chairman of Tata Sons. He was a chairman of the Tata Group from 1990 to 2012, and interim chairman from October 2016 through February 2017. He continues to head its charitable trusts In 2008, he received the Padma Vibhushan, the second highest civilian honour in India, 000TATA

Awards and Achievements

  1. Padma Bhushan: In 2000, Ratan Tata was honored with the Padma Bhushan, one of India's highest civilian awards, for his outstanding contributions to the industry.
  2. Padma Vibhushan: In 2008, he received the Padma Vibhushan, India's second-highest civilian award, recognizing his exceptional achievements in the fields of industry and business.
  3. Ernst & Young Entrepreneur of the Year: Ratan Tata was awarded the Ernst & Young Entrepreneur of the Year in 2003 for his leadership and vision in transforming the Tata Group.
  4. Lifetime Achievement Award: He received the Lifetime Achievement Award from the Rockefeller Foundation in 2012 for his commitment to corporate philanthropy and social responsibility.
  5. Carnegie Medal of Philanthropy: Ratan Tata was honored with the Carnegie Medal of Philanthropy in 2019 for his philanthropic efforts and contributions to society.
  6. Honorary Doctorates: He has received honorary doctorates from several universities and institutions, including the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Bombay, Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kharagpur, and the University of Cambridge.
  7. Honorary Citizenship: He was granted honorary citizenship of Singapore in recognition of his significant contributions to the business community.
  8. Honorary Knight Commander of the Order of the British Empire (KBE): In 2009, he was awarded the honorary KBE by the United Kingdom for his services to the business and the UK-India relations.
